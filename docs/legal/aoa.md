# §1 Name, Registered Office and Share Capital

1. The company shall bear the name **SSCCS gUG (with Limited Liability)**.
2. The registered office of the company is Berlin, Germany.
3. The share capital of the company amounts to 500 Euros.

# §2 Purpose of the Company and Charitable Status

1. The purpose of the company is the promotion of science and research as well as environmental protection **within the meaning of §§ 51 to 68 of the German Fiscal Code (Abgabenordnung - AO)**. Specifically, the company serves the following charitable purposes pursuant to § 52 para. 2 AO:

   a) **Promotion of science and research** (§ 52 para. 2 no. 1 AO) through the research, development and dissemination of the **State-Space Composition Computing System (SSCCS)**, a new energy-efficient computing paradigm.
   
   b) **Promotion of education** (§ 52 para. 2 no. 7 AO) through the creation of teaching materials, the conducting of workshops and the training of developers in the field of sustainable computer science.
   
   c) **Promotion of environmental protection** (§ 52 para. 2 no. 8 AO) through the development and provision of computing methods that significantly reduce energy consumption in compute-intensive tasks (particularly big data analyses in the public sector).

2. The company pursues exclusively and directly charitable purposes within the meaning of the section "Tax-Privileged Purposes" of the German Fiscal Code.
3. The company acts selflessly; it does not primarily pursue its own economic interests.

**SSCCS Note**
SSCCS is a computing system that treats state spaces as basic units and performs calculations through their composition. It implements a new computing paradigm through dynamic metaprogramming and can be executed on existing hardware and software, while potentially being expandable to research on optimized hardware, thereby creating foundations for industrial development and employment. A primary goal is to achieve maximum accuracy in big data analyses for purposes serving the public interest while simultaneously minimizing energy consumption. It is provided as open-source and can be used for research, education and global cooperation.

4. The company shall in particular pursue the following activities:
   a) Development and publication of open-source software
   b) Scientific research and publications
   c) Creation of educational materials and conducting of workshops
   d) Promotion of developer participation
   e) Support of charitable projects in resource planning and disaster prevention
   f) Research into SSCCS applications in extreme environments
   g) Promotion of international cooperation
   h) Research into energy-efficient computing methods for public institutions

# §3 Use of Funds

1. The funds of the company may only be used for the purposes set out in the Articles of Association.
2. No person may be favored by expenditures that are alien to the purpose of the company or by disproportionately high remuneration.
3. **The Managing Director shall generally perform his duties on an honorary basis. Appropriate remuneration within the framework of tax law requirements remains permissible.**

# §4 Asset Lock

1. In the event of dissolution of the company or loss of tax-privileged purposes, the assets of the company shall pass to a legal entity under public law or another tax-privileged corporation **for use in the promotion of science and research or environmental protection in accordance with § 55 AO**. The determination of the beneficiary corporation shall be the responsibility of the Shareholders' Meeting.

# §5 Organs of the Company

1. The organs of the company are the Shareholders' Meeting and the Managing Director.
2. The Shareholders' Meeting consists of all shareholders.
3. The Shareholders' Meeting is responsible for:
   a) Appointment and dismissal of the Managing Director
   b) Receipt of the annual financial statements and activity report
   c) Discharge of the Managing Director
   d) Amendments to the Articles of Association
   e) Decision on the dissolution of the company
   f) Determination of the beneficiary corporation in case of dissolution

4. The company has at least one Managing Director.
5. The Managing Director acts on an honorary basis; necessary expenses shall be reimbursed.
6. The term of office is 3 years; reappointment is possible.

# §6 Representation and Decision-Making

1. The Managing Director represents the company in and out of court.
2. For important decisions, the Managing Director may involve an advisory board.
3. Resolutions of the Shareholders' Meeting shall be passed by a simple majority of the votes cast, unless otherwise provided by law or the Articles of Association.

# §7 Accounting and Reporting Obligations

1. The financial year corresponds to the calendar year.
2. The Managing Director prepares annual financial statements as well as an activity report annually.
3. These documents shall be submitted to the Shareholders' Meeting.

# §8 Founders and Shareholders

1. The founders shall be entered in the Commercial Register.
2. The founders appoint the first Managing Director.
3. The company may admit additional shareholders.

# §9 Dissolution of the Company

1. Dissolution shall be effected by a resolution of the Shareholders' Meeting with a majority of three quarters of the votes.
2. In the event of dissolution, the Managing Director shall be the liquidator.
3. Upon dissolution, the assets shall be transferred in accordance with § 4.
